# Quick Reference Guide

## üìã Grammar Quick Reference

### Articles
- **A:** Before consonant sounds (a book, a university)
- **An:** Before vowel sounds (an apple, an hour)
- **The:** Specific nouns, superlatives, unique things

### Tenses - Key Indicators

**Present Simple:** every day, always, usually, often
- Structure: Subject + base verb (+ s/es)

**Present Continuous:** now, at the moment, currently
- Structure: Subject + is/am/are + verb + ing

**Present Perfect:** for, since, already, yet, just
- Structure: Subject + have/has + past participle

**Past Simple:** yesterday, last week, ago
- Structure: Subject + past form

**Past Continuous:** when, while, at that time
- Structure: Subject + was/were + verb + ing

**Past Perfect:** before, after, by the time
- Structure: Subject + had + past participle

**Future Simple:** tomorrow, next week, will
- Structure: Subject + will/shall + base verb

---

### Subject-Verb Agreement Rules

1. Singular subject ‚Üí singular verb
2. Plural subject ‚Üí plural verb
3. **Each/Every/Everyone** ‚Üí always singular
4. **Either...or/Neither...nor** ‚Üí verb agrees with nearest subject
5. **A number of** ‚Üí plural verb
6. **The number of** ‚Üí singular verb
7. **Collective nouns** ‚Üí usually singular

---

### Active to Passive Voice

**Present Simple:**
- Active: She writes letters.
- Passive: Letters are written by her.

**Past Simple:**
- Active: She wrote letters.
- Passive: Letters were written by her.

**Future Simple:**
- Active: She will write letters.
- Passive: Letters will be written by her.

---

### Direct to Indirect Speech Changes

**Tenses (if reporting verb is past):**
- Present ‚Üí Past
- Past ‚Üí Past Perfect
- Will ‚Üí Would

**Pronouns:**
- I ‚Üí he/she
- You ‚Üí I/he/she/they
- We ‚Üí they

**Time Words:**
- Now ‚Üí then
- Today ‚Üí that day
- Tomorrow ‚Üí the next day
- Yesterday ‚Üí the day before

## üìù Common Prepositions

### Time
- **At:** at 3 o'clock, at night
- **On:** on Monday, on January 1st
- **In:** in 2024, in the morning
- **For:** for 5 years (duration)

- **At:** at home, at school
- **On:** on the table, on the wall
- **In:** in the room, in Pakistan

### Common Patterns

## üìö Vocabulary Quick Reference

### Common Synonyms

**Academic:**
- Study: learn, research, examine

- Important: significant, crucial, essential
- Difficult: hard, challenging, tough

**Descriptive:**
- Big: large, huge, enormous

### Common Antonyms
- Big ‚Üî Small
- Good ‚Üî Bad
- Happy ‚Üî Sad
- Early ‚Üî Late
- Begin ‚Üî End

### Commonly Confused Words

**Accept/Except:**
- Accept (verb): receive
- Except (preposition): excluding

**Affect/Effect:**
- Affect (verb): influence
- Effect (noun): result

**Advice/Advise:**
- Advice (noun): suggestion
- Advise (verb): suggest

**Lose/Loose:**
- Lose (verb): misplace
- Loose (adjective): not tight

**Than/Then:**
- Than: comparison
- Then: time

**Their/There/They're:**
- Their: belonging to them
- There: place
- They're: they are

## üî§ Prefixes and Suffixes

### Common Prefixes
- **Un-/In-/Im-:** not (unhappy, incorrect, impossible)
- **Re-:** again (rewrite, rebuild)
- **Pre-:** before (preview, prefix)
- **Post-:** after (postwar, postgraduate)
- **Mis-:** wrong (misunderstand, misbehave)
- **Over-:** too much (overwork, oversleep)
- **Under-:** too little (underestimate, underpay)

### Common Suffixes
- **-er/-or:** person (teacher, actor)
- **-tion/-sion:** action (education, decision)
- **-ly:** adverb (quickly, carefully)
- **-ful:** full of (careful, hopeful)
- **-less:** without (hopeless, careless)
- **-able/-ible:** can be done (readable, possible)

## üìñ Reading Comprehension Strategies

### Steps:
1. **Skim:** Read quickly for main idea
2. **Scan:** Look for specific information
3. **Read Carefully:** For detailed questions
4. **Answer:** Based on passage, not outside knowledge

### Question Types:
- **Factual:** Directly stated information
- **Inference:** Implied information
- **Main Idea:** Central point
- **Vocabulary:** Word meaning in context
- **Purpose/Tone:** Author's intention or attitude

## ‚úçÔ∏è Writing Quick Reference

### Paragraph Structure:
1. **Topic Sentence:** Main idea
2. **Supporting Sentences:** Details, examples
3. **Concluding Sentence:** Summary

### Essay Structure:
1. **Introduction:** Hook + Background + Thesis
2. **Body:** 2-3 paragraphs with topic sentences
3. **Conclusion:** Restate thesis + Summary + Final thought

### Common Errors to Avoid:
- Run-on sentences
- Fragment sentences
- Subject-verb disagreement
- Wrong prepositions
- Spelling mistakes
- Tense inconsistency

## ‚è±Ô∏è Test-Taking Tips

### Time Management:
- Allocate time per question type
- Don't spend more than 1 minute per question initially
- Leave 5 minutes for review

### Strategies:
1. Read instructions carefully
2. Answer easy questions first
3. Eliminate wrong answers
4. Don't leave questions blank (if no negative marking)
5. Stay calm and focused

### Common Mistakes:
- Not reading questions carefully
- Spending too much time on one question
- Changing answers unnecessarily
- Not managing time
- Panicking
