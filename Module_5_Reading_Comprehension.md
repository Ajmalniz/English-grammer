## Chapter 1: Reading Strategies

### Skimming
**Skimming** means reading quickly to get the main idea.

**How to Skim:**
1. Read the title and headings
2. Read the first and last sentences of paragraphs
3. Look for keywords
4. Don't read every word

**When to Use:** To get a general understanding quickly

---

### Scanning
**Scanning** means looking for specific information.

**How to Scan:**
1. Know what you're looking for
2. Move your eyes quickly
3. Look for keywords, numbers, names
4. Stop when you find the information

**When to Use:** To find specific facts or details

---

### Active Reading
**Active Reading** means reading with focus and purpose.

**Steps:**
1. **Preview:** Look at title, headings, first paragraph
2. **Question:** Ask what you want to learn
3. **Read:** Read carefully, noting important points
4. **Review:** Summarize what you read

---

## Chapter 2: Main Ideas and Supporting Details

### Main Idea
The **main idea** is the central point of a passage.

**How to Find Main Idea:**
- Usually in the first or last sentence
- Look for repeated concepts
- Ask: "What is this passage mainly about?"

**Example:**
"Regular exercise has many benefits. It improves physical health, strengthens muscles, and boosts mental well-being. Exercise also helps reduce stress and improves sleep quality."

**Main Idea:** Regular exercise has many benefits.

---

### Supporting Details
**Supporting details** explain or prove the main idea.

**Types of Supporting Details:**
- Examples
- Facts and statistics
- Reasons
- Descriptions

**Example (from above):**
- Detail 1: Improves physical health
- Detail 2: Strengthens muscles
- Detail 3: Boosts mental well-being
- Detail 4: Reduces stress
- Detail 5: Improves sleep quality

---

## Chapter 3: Making Inferences

### What is an Inference?
An **inference** is a conclusion based on evidence and reasoning, not directly stated.

**How to Make Inferences:**
1. Read carefully
2. Look for clues
3. Use your knowledge
4. Draw logical conclusions

**Example:**
"Ahmed arrived at the exam hall at 9:00 AM. The exam started at 8:30 AM. He looked worried and was breathing heavily."

**Inference:** Ahmed was late for the exam and was worried about it.

---

### Inference Questions
Common question types:
- "What can we infer about..."
- "The passage suggests that..."
- "It is likely that..."
- "The author implies that..."

---

## Chapter 4: Author's Purpose and Tone

### Author's Purpose
**Why did the author write this?**

**Common Purposes:**
1. **Inform:** To give information
2. **Persuade:** To convince the reader
3. **Entertain:** To amuse or interest
4. **Explain:** To make something clear

**Clues:**
- **Inform:** Facts, statistics, explanations
- **Persuade:** Opinions, arguments, "should," "must"
- **Entertain:** Stories, jokes, interesting details
- **Explain:** "How to," step-by-step instructions

---

### Author's Tone
**Tone** is the author's attitude toward the subject.

**Common Tones:**
- **Formal:** Serious, professional
- **Informal:** Casual, friendly
- **Positive:** Optimistic, encouraging
- **Negative:** Critical, pessimistic
- **Neutral:** Objective, balanced
- **Humorous:** Funny, light

**Clues:**
- Word choice
- Sentence structure
- Examples used

---

## Chapter 5: Types of Comprehension Questions

### Type 1: Factual Questions
Ask about directly stated information.

**Example:**
"What time did the exam start?"
- Answer is directly in the passage

---

### Type 2: Inference Questions
Ask about implied information.

**Example:**
"What can we infer about Ahmed's feelings?"
- Answer requires reasoning

---

### Type 3: Vocabulary Questions
Ask about word meanings in context.

**Example:**
"What does 'elated' mean in this passage?"
- Use context clues

---

### Type 4: Main Idea Questions
Ask about the central point.

**Example:**
"What is the main idea of this passage?"
- Look for the central theme

---

### Type 5: Purpose/Tone Questions
Ask about author's intention or attitude.

**Example:**
"What is the author's purpose?"
- Identify why the author wrote it

### Practice Passage 1

**Read the following passage and answer the questions:**

"Education is the foundation of personal and societal development. Through education, individuals gain knowledge, develop critical thinking skills, and learn to solve problems. Schools and universities provide structured learning environments where students can explore various subjects. Moreover, education opens doors to better career opportunities and higher income. It also promotes social equality by giving everyone a chance to succeed regardless of their background."

**Questions:**

1. **What is the main idea of this passage?**
   a) Schools are important
   b) Education has many benefits
   c) Students learn many subjects
   d) Education is expensive

2. **According to the passage, what does education help individuals gain?**
   a) Money only
   b) Knowledge and skills
   c) Friends only
   d) Jobs only

3. **What can we infer about education from this passage?**
   a) It is only for rich people
   b) It is important for everyone
   c) It is not necessary
   d) It is only for children

**Answers:**
1. b) Education has many benefits
2. b) Knowledge and skills
3. b) It is important for everyone
