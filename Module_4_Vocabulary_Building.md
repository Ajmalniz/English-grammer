## Chapter 2: Word Meanings in Context

### Understanding Context Clues

#### Type 1: Definition Clues
The word is defined in the sentence.
- Example: **Procrastination**, which means delaying tasks, is a bad habit.

#### Type 2: Example Clues
Examples help understand the word.
- Example: **Mammals** such as dogs, cats, and humans are warm-blooded.

#### Type 3: Synonym Clues
Similar words are used nearby.
- Example: She was **elated**, very happy about her success.

#### Type 4: Antonym Clues
Opposite words help understand meaning.
- Example: Unlike his **extroverted** brother, he was very shy.

#### Type 5: Inference Clues
You infer meaning from the situation.
- Example: After studying all night, he felt **exhausted**.

## Chapter 5: Commonly Confused Words

### Word Pairs Often Confused

#### 1. **Accept / Except**
- **Accept:** receive (verb)
  - I **accept** your offer.
- **Except:** excluding (preposition)
  - Everyone came **except** Ali.

#### 2. **Affect / Effect**
- **Affect:** influence (verb)
  - Rain will **affect** the match.
- **Effect:** result (noun)
  - The **effect** of rain was cancellation.

#### 3. **Advice / Advise**
- **Advice:** suggestion (noun, uncountable)
  - She gave me good **advice**.
- **Advise:** suggest (verb)
  - She **advised** me to study.

#### 4. **Lose / Loose**
- **Lose:** misplace/fail (verb)
  - Don't **lose** your book.
- **Loose:** not tight (adjective)
  - The shirt is **loose**.

#### 5. **Than / Then**
- **Than:** comparison
  - She is taller **than** him.
- **Then:** time
  - First study, **then** play.

#### 6. **Their / There / They're**
- **Their:** belonging to them
  - **Their** books are here.
- **There:** place
  - Put it **there**.
- **They're:** they are
  - **They're** studying.

#### 7. **Your / You're**
- **Your:** belonging to you
  - **Your** book is here.
- **You're:** you are
  - **You're** intelligent.

#### 8. **Its / It's**
- **Its:** belonging to it
  - The dog wagged **its** tail.
- **It's:** it is
  - **It's** raining.

#### 9. **Principal / Principle**
- **Principal:** main/head of school
  - The **principal** spoke.
- **Principle:** rule/belief
  - It's a matter of **principle**.

#### 10. **Stationary / Stationery**
- **Stationary:** not moving
  - The car was **stationary**.
- **Stationery:** writing materials
  - Buy **stationery** for school.
