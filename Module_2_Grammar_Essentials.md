## Chapter 1: Tenses - Present Tense

### Present Simple Tense

**Structure:** Subject + Base Verb (+ s/es for third person)

**Uses:**
- Habitual actions: I **study** every day.
- General truths: The sun **rises** in the east.
- Scheduled events: The train **leaves** at 9 AM.

**Third Person Singular Rules:**
- Add -s: study ‚Üí studies, play ‚Üí plays
- Add -es: go ‚Üí goes, watch ‚Üí watches, fix ‚Üí fixes
- Change -y to -ies: study ‚Üí studies, try ‚Üí tries

**Examples:**
- I **study** English.
- She **studies** English.
- They **study** English.

---

### Present Continuous Tense

**Structure:** Subject + is/am/are + Verb + ing

**Uses:**
- Actions happening now: I **am studying** now.
- Temporary actions: She **is living** in Karachi this month.
- Future plans: We **are meeting** tomorrow.

**Examples:**
- I **am reading** a book.
- He **is writing** an essay.
- They **are playing** cricket.

---

### Present Perfect Tense

**Structure:** Subject + have/has + Past Participle

**Uses:**
- Completed actions with present relevance: I **have finished** my homework.
- Life experiences: She **has visited** Lahore.
- Actions started in past, continuing: I **have lived** here for 5 years.

**Examples:**
- I **have completed** the assignment.
- He **has seen** that movie.
- They **have been** friends since childhood.

---

### Present Perfect Continuous Tense

**Structure:** Subject + have/has + been + Verb + ing

**Uses:**
- Actions started in past, still continuing: I **have been studying** for 2 hours.
- Emphasize duration: She **has been waiting** since morning.

**Examples:**
- I **have been reading** this book for a week.
- He **has been working** here since 2020.

---

## Chapter 2: Tenses - Past Tense

### Past Simple Tense

**Structure:** Subject + Past Form of Verb

**Uses:**
- Completed actions in past: I **studied** yesterday.
- Past habits: She **walked** to school every day.
- Specific time in past: We **met** last week.

**Regular Verbs:** Add -ed (walk ‚Üí walked, study ‚Üí studied)
**Irregular Verbs:** Change form (go ‚Üí went, write ‚Üí wrote, see ‚Üí saw)

**Examples:**
- I **went** to the library yesterday.
- She **wrote** a letter last night.
- They **studied** together.

---

### Past Continuous Tense

**Structure:** Subject + was/were + Verb + ing

**Uses:**
- Actions in progress at specific past time: I **was studying** at 8 PM yesterday.
- Interrupted actions: I **was reading** when she called.
- Two simultaneous actions: While I **was studying**, she **was cooking**.

**Examples:**
- I **was watching** TV when the phone rang.
- They **were playing** cricket yesterday evening.

---

### Past Perfect Tense

**Structure:** Subject + had + Past Participle

**Uses:**
- Action completed before another past action: I **had finished** my homework before she arrived.
- Show sequence: After I **had eaten**, I went out.

**Examples:**
- She **had left** before I reached.
- They **had completed** the project by then.

---

### Past Perfect Continuous Tense

**Structure:** Subject + had + been + Verb + ing

**Uses:**
- Duration before another past action: I **had been studying** for 2 hours when she called.

**Examples:**
- He **had been waiting** for an hour before the bus arrived.

---

## Chapter 3: Tenses - Future Tense

### Future Simple Tense

**Structure:** Subject + will/shall + Base Verb

**Uses:**
- Future actions: I **will study** tomorrow.
- Predictions: It **will rain** today.
- Promises: I **will help** you.

**Examples:**
- I **will complete** this task.
- She **will come** tomorrow.

---

### Future Continuous Tense

**Structure:** Subject + will/shall + be + Verb + ing

**Uses:**
- Actions in progress at future time: I **will be studying** at 8 PM tomorrow.

**Examples:**
- They **will be waiting** for you.

---

### Future Perfect Tense

**Structure:** Subject + will/shall + have + Past Participle

**Uses:**
- Actions completed before future time: I **will have finished** by 5 PM.

**Examples:**
- She **will have completed** her studies by next year.

---

### Future Perfect Continuous Tense

**Structure:** Subject + will/shall + have + been + Verb + ing

**Uses:**
- Duration up to future time: By next month, I **will have been living** here for a year.

**Examples:**
- They **will have been working** for 10 hours by evening.

---

## Chapter 4: Subject-Verb Agreement

### Basic Rules

**Rule 1: Singular subject takes singular verb**
- The student **studies** hard.
- A book **is** on the table.

**Rule 2: Plural subject takes plural verb**
- The students **study** hard.
- Books **are** on the table.

---

### Special Cases

#### 1. **Collective Nouns**
- Usually singular: The team **is** playing. (team as one unit)
- Can be plural: The team **are** arguing. (team members individually)

#### 2. **Either...or / Neither...nor**
- Verb agrees with nearest subject:
  - Either Ali or his friends **are** coming.
  - Neither the students nor the teacher **is** present.

#### 3. **Each, Every, Everyone, Someone**
- Always singular:
  - Each student **has** a book.
  - Everyone **is** present.

#### 4. **There is / There are**
- Agrees with the noun after it:
  - There **is** a book on the table.
  - There **are** books on the table.

#### 5. **A number of / The number of**
- A number of students **are** present. (plural)
- The number of students **is** increasing. (singular)

#### 6. **Subjects joined by 'and'**
- Usually plural: Ali and Ahmed **are** friends.
- Exception (same person/thing): Bread and butter **is** my breakfast.

#### 7. **Uncountable Nouns**
- Always singular: Water **is** important. Information **is** valuable.

---

## Chapter 5: Active and Passive Voice

### Active Voice
**Structure:** Subject + Verb + Object
- The subject does the action.
- Example: The teacher **teaches** the students.

### Passive Voice
**Structure:** Object + is/am/are/was/were + Past Participle + by + Subject
- The subject receives the action.
- Example: The students **are taught** by the teacher.

---

### Tense Changes in Passive Voice

#### Present Simple
- Active: She **writes** letters.
- Passive: Letters **are written** by her.

#### Present Continuous
- Active: She **is writing** letters.
- Passive: Letters **are being written** by her.

#### Present Perfect
- Active: She **has written** letters.
- Passive: Letters **have been written** by her.

#### Past Simple
- Active: She **wrote** letters.
- Passive: Letters **were written** by her.

#### Past Continuous
- Active: She **was writing** letters.
- Passive: Letters **were being written** by her.

#### Past Perfect
- Active: She **had written** letters.
- Passive: Letters **had been written** by her.

#### Future Simple
- Active: She **will write** letters.
- Passive: Letters **will be written** by her.

---

### When to Use Passive Voice
- When the doer is unknown: The window **was broken**.
- When the doer is obvious: English **is spoken** worldwide.
- When focus is on action, not doer: The test **was conducted** yesterday.

---

## Chapter 6: Direct and Indirect Speech

### Direct Speech
- Exact words spoken, in quotation marks.
- Example: She said, "I am studying."

### Indirect Speech
- Reported words, no quotation marks.
- Example: She said that she **was** studying.

---

### Changes in Indirect Speech

#### 1. **Pronouns**
- I ‚Üí he/she
- You ‚Üí I/he/she/they
- We ‚Üí they
- Example: He said, "I am tired." ‚Üí He said that **he** was tired.

#### 2. **Tenses** (if reporting verb is past)
- Present Simple ‚Üí Past Simple: "I study" ‚Üí he **studied**
- Present Continuous ‚Üí Past Continuous: "I am studying" ‚Üí he **was studying**
- Present Perfect ‚Üí Past Perfect: "I have studied" ‚Üí he **had studied**
- Past Simple ‚Üí Past Perfect: "I studied" ‚Üí he **had studied**
- Will ‚Üí Would: "I will study" ‚Üí he **would** study

**Note:** If reporting verb is present/future, tenses don't change.

#### 3. **Time and Place Words**
- Now ‚Üí then
- Today ‚Üí that day
- Tomorrow ‚Üí the next day
- Yesterday ‚Üí the day before
- Here ‚Üí there
- This ‚Üí that
- These ‚Üí those

#### 4. **Reporting Verbs**
- Said ‚Üí said that
- Says ‚Üí says that
- Asked ‚Üí asked (for questions)
- Told ‚Üí told (with object)

---

### Examples

**Statements:**
- Direct: He said, "I am studying."
- Indirect: He said that he **was** studying.

**Questions:**
- Direct: She asked, "Are you studying?"
- Indirect: She asked if I **was** studying.
- Direct: He asked, "What are you doing?"
- Indirect: He asked what I **was** doing.

**Commands:**
- Direct: He said, "Study hard."
- Indirect: He told me **to study** hard.
- Direct: She said, "Don't waste time."
- Indirect: She told me **not to waste** time.
