## Chapter 1: Types of Sentences

### 1. Simple Sentence
- Contains one independent clause (one subject + one predicate)
- Example: **She studies hard.**

**Structure:** Subject + Verb + (Object)

**More Examples:**
- The student reads books.
- Ali and Ahmed play cricket. (compound subject)
- She reads and writes. (compound predicate)

---

### 2. Compound Sentence
- Contains two or more independent clauses joined by coordinating conjunctions
- Example: **She studies hard, and she passes the test.**

**Conjunctions:** and, but, or, nor, for, yet, so

**More Examples:**
- I studied hard, **but** I failed the test.
- She can study now, **or** she can study later.
- He was tired, **so** he went to sleep.

---

### 3. Complex Sentence
- Contains one independent clause + one or more dependent clauses
- Example: **She studies hard because she wants to pass.**

**Subordinating Conjunctions:** because, although, if, when, while, since, until, unless, after, before

**More Examples:**
- **Although** she studied hard, she failed.
- I will help you **if** you ask me.
- **When** she arrived, the class had started.

---

### 4. Compound-Complex Sentence
- Contains two or more independent clauses + one or more dependent clauses
- Example: **She studies hard because she wants to pass, and she always succeeds.**

**More Examples:**
- **When** I arrived, she was studying, **and** she didn't notice me.
- **Although** it was raining, we went out, **but** we got wet.

## Chapter 2: Sentence Correction

### Common Errors and Corrections

#### Error 1: Subject-Verb Disagreement
- **Wrong:** The students was studying.
- **Correct:** The students **were** studying.

#### Error 2: Wrong Tense
- **Wrong:** I go to Lahore yesterday.
- **Correct:** I **went** to Lahore yesterday.

#### Error 3: Double Negative
- **Wrong:** I don't have no books.
- **Correct:** I don't have **any** books. OR I have **no** books.

#### Error 4: Wrong Preposition
- **Wrong:** She is good in English.
- **Correct:** She is good **at** English.

#### Error 5: Missing Article
- **Wrong:** She is honest person.
- **Correct:** She is **an** honest person.

#### Error 6: Wrong Pronoun
- **Wrong:** Between you and I, this is difficult.
- **Correct:** Between you and **me**, this is difficult.

#### Error 7: Incomplete Comparison
- **Wrong:** She is more intelligent.
- **Correct:** She is more intelligent **than her sister**.

#### Error 8: Run-on Sentence
- **Wrong:** She studies hard she passes the test.
- **Correct:** She studies hard, **so** she passes the test.
- **OR:** She studies hard. She passes the test.

#### Error 9: Fragment Sentence
- **Wrong:** Because she studied hard.
- **Correct:** She passed **because** she studied hard.

#### Error 10: Wrong Word Order
- **Wrong:** Always I study at night.
- **Correct:** I **always** study at night.

## Chapter 3: Sentence Completion

### Types of Sentence Completion Questions

#### Type 1: Grammar-Based Completion
**Example:**
She _____ English for five years.
a) study
b) studies
c) has been studying
d) studied

**Answer: c) has been studying** (duration from past to present)

---

#### Type 2: Vocabulary-Based Completion
**Example:**
She is very _____ in mathematics.
a) good
b) well
c) better
d) best

**Answer: a) good** (adjective needed, 'well' is adverb)

---

#### Type 3: Preposition-Based Completion
**Example:**
She is interested _____ science.
a) in
b) at
c) on
d) for

**Answer: a) in** (interested in)

---

#### Type 4: Conjunction-Based Completion
**Example:**
She studied hard _____ she passed the test.
a) but
b) so
c) or
d) yet

**Answer: b) so** (shows result)

---

### Common Patterns for Sentence Completion

#### Pattern 1: Conditional Sentences
- **If** + present, **will** + base verb: If she studies, she **will** pass.
- **If** + past, **would** + base verb: If she studied, she **would** pass.
- **If** + past perfect, **would have** + past participle: If she had studied, she **would have** passed.

#### Pattern 2: Time Clauses
- **When/While/After/Before** + clause: **When** she arrived, the class had started.
- **By the time** + clause: **By the time** we reached, the train had left.

#### Pattern 3: Reason Clauses
- **Because/Since/As** + clause: She passed **because** she studied hard.

#### Pattern 4: Contrast Clauses
- **Although/Though/Even though** + clause: **Although** she studied hard, she failed.
- **But/However:** She studied hard, **but** she failed.

## Chapter 4: Common Sentence Errors in Entry Tests

### Error Category 1: Articles
- Missing article: She is **a** student. (not "She is student")
- Wrong article: **An** hour (not "A hour")
- Unnecessary article: I like **music**. (not "I like the music" - general)

### Error Category 2: Prepositions
- Wrong preposition: good **at** (not "good in")
- Missing preposition: interested **in** (not "interested")
- Wrong preposition: arrive **at** (place), arrive **in** (city/country)

### Error Category 3: Tenses
- Wrong tense: I **went** yesterday (not "I go yesterday")
- Inconsistent tense: She **studied** hard and **passed** (not "studies and passed")

### Error Category 4: Pronouns
- Wrong case: between you and **me** (not "between you and I")
- Wrong reference: **Each** student should bring **his/her** book (not "their")

### Error Category 5: Modifiers
- Misplaced modifier: **Only** she can help (not "She can only help" - changes meaning)
- Dangling modifier: **Walking** to school, I saw a bird (not "Walking to school, a bird was seen")

## Chapter 5: Parallel Structure

### What is Parallel Structure?
Items in a list should have the same grammatical form.

### Examples

**Wrong (Not Parallel):**
- She likes **reading**, **writing**, and **to play**.
- He is **tall**, **intelligent**, and **works** hard.

**Correct (Parallel):**
- She likes **reading**, **writing**, and **playing**.
- He is **tall**, **intelligent**, and **hardworking**.

### Common Parallel Structures

#### 1. Lists
- **Wrong:** I like to read, writing, and to play.
- **Correct:** I like **to read**, **to write**, and **to play**.
- **OR:** I like **reading**, **writing**, and **playing**.

#### 2. Correlative Conjunctions
- **Wrong:** She both **studies** hard and **is playing** cricket.
- **Correct:** She both **studies** hard and **plays** cricket.

#### 3. Comparisons
- **Wrong:** She is better **at reading** than **to write**.
- **Correct:** She is better **at reading** than **at writing**.
